Project 6
Concept for a Community History Website
I designed this small selection of wireframes and mockups to showcase a concept for a historically-themed website, which acts as an archival resource and historical society for Excelsior Springs, Missouri. This project simply provides an example of some of the mockups I've designed. No human factors research (e.g. walkthroughs, expert reviews, usability tests etc.) was conducted.

The image gallery portion of the ‘Resources’ menu item. Users may arrive here through the top navigation bar, or by clicking on individual images from the map.
This portion of the website allows users to engage with eachother on subjects related to Excelsior Springs history and the club. The ‘Event Calendar’ button leads to a page that lays out upcoming club meetings while the ‘Meeting Notes’ button navigates to a page consisting of recorded lectures and transcripts. The panel on the left is an instant messaging platform that has been divided into separate topics or subsections. Clicking the ‘back arrow’ in the header of the messaging platform brings the user to a menu of all the different topics of conversation.
