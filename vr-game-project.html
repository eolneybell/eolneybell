Project 4
Educational Game in Virtual Reality
The goal of this project (involving me and two other human factors students) was to design and develop a physics lesson in virtual reality, and to conduct a study measuring its effectiveness at teaching this lesson. To gain guidance on the teaching effectiveness of gamified lessons, we conducted an expert review of online physics and virtual reality games. We wrote and administered a questionnaire to introductory physics students, developed personas in order to get into our target usersâ€™ shoes, and eventually conducted tests with our game to measure its usability and educational value.

After deriving the need for an educational game through surveys taken by students at Tufts University, we looked to existing physics games for inspiration.
A sampling of the user needs and requirements we came up with. Our group also conducted cognitive walkthroughs, evaluations on several iterations of our own game, and recommendations for improvments to our game.

I conducted tests for both the game's usability and its educational value. The latter consisted of pre- and post-tests administered to participants in two groups: a VR gamified lesson condition and a more traditional video lecture condition.
Test moderators used a monitor to observe what the participant was seeing through their headset
